Colors

This page is here to teach anyone about colors and how to use them in the world of web and software design. To help people understand two types of colors: Primary colors and Secondary colors. After viewing this web page, any person, if given a can of paint from each secondary color, should be able to explain which primary colors were mixed together to create each secondary color


Primary Colors

The building blocks from which all other colors are derived. Also known as basic colors, as they canâ€™t be recreated by color mixing, traditional art and color theory accept RYB as the primary colors. As humans are trichromatic, RYB is fundamental to see the color spectrum of our world.


The Primary Colors are:

  • RED
  • BLUE
  • YELLOW

Secondary Colors

These are color combinations created by the equal mixture of two primary colors. On the color wheel, secondary colors are located between primary colors. According to the traditional color wheel, red and yellow make orange, red and blue make purple, and blue and yellow make green.


The Secondary Colors are:

  • ORANGE
  • GREEN
  • PURPLE

Primary and Secondary

Primary colors are considered the root of every other color. The Secondary colors cannot exist without primary colors. A combination of primary as well as secondary colors create Tertiary colors. Some of these Tertiary colors include blue and purple , green and yellow , red and orange , blue and green , and yellow and orange .
